CEntertain

aka. Tim Gerullat

I'm a freelance and hobby 3D, graphic, and web designer.

MOTIVES

All my work is lovingly hand made (as well as this website). And my tools of choice are, depending on the job, Blender, Paint.NET, Visual Studio, VSDC, and Google Docs.
When working on a project of any kind, inspiration is key. The reasons I get motivated to realize something are often quite simple. Someone presents an interesting idea, someone needs something specific realized, there is something that pops up on a feed, or there's something astonishing in nature. Little details matter. I then often try to capture the idea of it, and what makes it so interesting, and break it down to a core. This is often helped with browsing through platforms like Pinterest or other image boards to get a sense of continuity, or to see what would be a nice addition to the final design.
Taking this website, for example, I wanted to capture this sense of flow and plain, clean shapes, combined with elements like shadows, and inverted lights to create something that's essentially eye candy. And that's really what you want to present an idea - or a product.

ROUTINE

The work cycle of a project is quite simple - Collect references and details on how it should look, combine the two, and then start shaping what comes into mind. The rest is essentially just pulling every trick in the book to put a personal spin on the whole thing and to increase the quality to the highest amount possible.
Of course, let's not forget the back and forth between me and my clients. And by that, I mean: I often push out updates about the current design's progress, receive feedback, and change or add details accordingly. It's a very honest process, and one that I kind of like, because it is a very collaborative way of going about a project, and one in which both parties can first of all see actual progress and how things are, but seconly also get to know each other better. Obviously, I am not saying that deep friendship necessarily has to start forming, but it's always better to have nice relations between you and the people you work with. It creates a generally nicer atmosphere, and that's really what drives the process forward as a whole. And let's be honest - Who wants to work with someone who's hysteric all the time?

HISTORY

Born in 2002. Grew up in a town in north western Germany. In 2021, I started with both, design and 3D modelling. Only an interest in the topic at the time, but it quickly became something more. It all started out with a small plan. And that was to simulate breaking glass, surprisingly.
Ever since the beginnings of my interests in design and art, there has been this sort of interest of mine in clean looking and sharp visuals. And I think this is the main contributing factor to why I like the kind of materials I often use. Materials and objects such as concrete, metal, water, plants, sand, glass, and plastic. They form this sort of concoction that is just plain nice to look at when combined under the right light. It is this interest in the topic as a whole that brought me to the point I am at right now, and the reason I am confident in saying that I feel at home around any kind of clean modern marketing design or design in general.

If you break it down, it is simple to create things people like to look at - It only comes down to knowing the details, and recognising beauty.
